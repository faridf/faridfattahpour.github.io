About Me

Hi, I'm Farid Fattahpour, a Ph.D. candidate specializing in materials transformation through advanced modeling and synthesis techniques. My research interests include high-entropy oxides, diffusion kinetics, and computational material science. I have a strong foundation in chemical engineering principles and problem-solving, and I’m passionate about leveraging interdisciplinary skills to innovate and enhance industrial processes.

I am currently working on predicting and understanding the high-pressure structural evolution of complex oxide materials, with a focus on enhancing their performance for industrial applications.

Skills

  • Programming Languages: Python, Bash, MATLAB
  • Machine Learning: Gaussian Process Regression (GPyTorch), Data Analysis (pandas), Scikit-Learn
  • Materials Characterization: FT-IR, DSC, TGA, NMR, UV-Vis, SEM, TEM, LC-MS, XRD, Raman, EDX Mapping
  • Synthesis Techniques: Organic Synthesis, Polymerization (Free-Radical, Condensation, ATRP), Nanomaterial Synthesis
  • Simulation & Modeling: DFT (VASP), Phonon Calculations (Phonopy), Molecular Dynamics, Wulff Construction
  • Software & Tools: ThermoCalc, VESTA, ChemDraw, Microsoft Office Suite

Projects

  • Resiliency and Morphology of High-Entropy Oxide Material: Predicted crystal morphology using the Wulff construction. Developed models to understand the structural transformations under varying conditions.
  • Heat Radiation Mitigation in Pyrosilicate Composites: Modeled heat radiation effects and refractive index properties in rare-earth pyrosilicate composites using DFT.
  • Improving Ab Initio Diffusion Calculations: Applied machine learning techniques for predicting diffusion in materials and locating transition states. Improved accuracy of diffusion rate predictions in complex structures.
  • Thermoplastic Polyurethane Synthesis: Synthesized a novel polyurethane with controllable degradation properties for biomedical applications.
